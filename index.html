About Me

Hi! I am robotics MSc student at ETH and a research assistant at the Robotic Systems Lab . I am passionate about impact-driven robotics through development of novel robot systems and advanced computational algorithms to tackle challenges innate to the mission of interest. In other words, I believe in the deployment of robots for humanitarian and environmental impact over commercial applications.

This is the motivation behind my interest in legged robotics, due to their ability to go (almost) anywhere.

I got my Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology 🐝

Exploiting structure to make optimization more tractable
Jointly optimizing over all decision variables with a single optimization is desirable and necessary to obtain a solution that is (locally) optimal but often not tractable at control rates (> 10 Hz). An attractive alternative is under mild assumptions to decompose the optimization into smaller problems that interact through a compact selection of quantities that captures the main effects of the decision variables. Solve the smaller problems until consensus and voila you have an optimization loop that is tractable at control rates!
